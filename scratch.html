Lake Michigan has a wide range of boating.

Boating in Door County, Wisconsin, is a serene and breathtaking experience. The crystal-clear waters of Green Bay and Lake Michigan offer the perfect backdrop for leisurely cruises or more adventurous excursions. As you glide through the waters, you'll pass by stunning shorelines, lush forests, and charming lighthouses. Many boaters enjoy exploring the small, picturesque islands, like Washington Island, or stopping to dock at one of the quaint harbor towns for a bite to eat. With its mix of natural beauty and tranquil atmosphere, Door County provides an unforgettable boating escape. The 1969 Boston Whaler Nauset is a classic example of durable, versatile boat design that has stood the test of time. Built with the iconic unsinkable foam-filled hull that made Boston Whaler famous, the Nauset features a center console layout with a mahogany pilot seat and windshield frame, giving it a timeless, rugged charm. At 16 feet 7 inches long, it was perfect for fishing, cruising, or utility use, and its lightweight design made it easy to trailer and launch. Owners appreciated its reliability and seaworthiness, especially in choppy waters, making it a favorite among recreational boaters and professionals alike.
